## 📖 LESSON 1 — What is the Stock Market?

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

Think about this: You have a brilliant business idea — say, a chai stall chain across Tamil Nadu.
You need ₹50 lakhs to open 10 stalls. But you only have ₹5 lakhs.

**What do you do?**

You can:
1. Take a **loan** from a bank (Debt)
2. Ask people to **co-own** your business by giving them a share of it (Equity)

The **Stock Market** is the organised, regulated place where option 2 happens — at massive scale.

### 📌 Key Definition:

> **Stock Market** = A marketplace where **shares (ownership units)** of publicly listed companies are **bought and sold** between investors.

### 🔑 Key Points:
- A **Share / Stock** = One unit of ownership in a company
- When you buy 1 share of TCS, you literally own a tiny piece of TCS
- The stock market has two sides:
  - **Primary Market** — Company sells shares for the first time (IPO)
  - **Secondary Market** — Investors trade shares among themselves (daily stock market)

## 📖 LESSON 2 — NSE & BSE: India's Two Stock Exchanges

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

India has **two major stock exchanges**. Think of them like two different shopping malls where stocks are "sold."

| Feature | BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) | NSE (National Stock Exchange) |
|---------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Founded** | 1875 (Asia's oldest!) | 1992 |
| **Location** | Mumbai | Mumbai |
| **Index** | **SENSEX** (Top 30 companies) | **NIFTY 50** (Top 50 companies) |
| **Listed Companies** | ~5,500+ | ~2,000+ |
| **Trading Volume** | Lower | **Higher** (most trades happen here) |

### 🔑 Key Points:
- Most serious investors track **NIFTY 50** as the market pulse
- **SENSEX** going up = market is bullish (positive mood)
- **SENSEX** going down = market is bearish (negative mood)
- Both exchanges trade the **same stocks** — prices are nearly identical

## 📖 LESSON 3 — SEBI: The Market Regulator

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

Imagine cricket without an umpire. Players would cheat, argue, and chaos would rule.
**SEBI** is the umpire of the Indian stock market.

> **SEBI = Securities and Exchange Board of India**
> Established: **1992**
> Purpose: **Protect investors & regulate the market**

### What does SEBI do?
- ✅ Prevents insider trading (using secret company info to profit)
- ✅ Ensures companies disclose financial results honestly
- ✅ Regulates brokers, mutual funds, and FIIs
- ✅ Investigates market frauds and scams
- ✅ Sets rules for IPOs

### 🔑 Key Takeaway:
> SEBI protects **you** — the investor. Without SEBI, big players would manipulate prices and retail investors would always lose.

## 📖 LESSON 4 — Equity vs Debt

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

There are only **two ways** a company raises money. Understanding this is FUNDAMENTAL.

| Feature | **Equity (Shares)** | **Debt (Bonds/Loans)** |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **You become** | Part-owner of the company | Lender to the company |
| **Returns** | Dividends + Capital Gain (variable) | Fixed interest |
| **Risk** | Higher risk, higher reward | Lower risk, lower reward |
| **Repayment** | No obligation to repay | Must repay principal + interest |
| **Example** | Buying TCS shares | Buying Government bonds |

### 🔑 Key Rule:
> **Equity = Own It | Debt = Loan It**
>
> As an investor, when you buy stocks — you are an **equity investor** (part owner).

## 📖 LESSON 5 — What is an IPO?

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

IPO = **Initial Public Offering**

When a **private company** decides to list on the stock exchange and sell shares to the **general public for the first time** — that event is called an IPO.

### The IPO Journey:
```
Private Company  →  SEBI Approval  →  IPO Launch  →  Listed on NSE/BSE  →  Public Trading
```

### Why do companies do IPOs?
- Raise large capital for expansion
- Early investors / promoters get to exit and cash out
- Brand credibility increases

### Key IPO Terms:
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Price Band** | Min-Max price range set for IPO (e.g., ₹400–₹420) |
| **Grey Market Premium (GMP)** | Unofficial pre-listing demand signal |
| **Allotment** | Whether you received shares or not |
| **Listing Day** | First day shares trade on exchange |

## 📖 LESSON 6 — Demat Account: Your Digital Share Locker

### 🧑‍🏫 Master Says:

In the old days, when you bought shares, you received a **physical paper certificate**.
That was a nightmare to store, transfer, and verify.

**Demat Account** = **Dematerialised Account** = Your **digital locker for shares**

> Just like your bank account holds money digitally, a **Demat account holds your shares digitally**.

### Key Players:
| Entity | Role |
|--------|------|
| **CDSL / NSDL** | Depositories that actually hold shares |
| **Broker** (Zerodha, Groww, Angel) | Interface to buy/sell |
| **Your Demat Account** | Where your purchased shares sit |

### To Start Investing, You Need:
1. ✅ **PAN Card** (mandatory)
2. ✅ **Aadhaar** (for KYC)
3. ✅ **Bank Account** (linked to demat)
4. ✅ **Open Demat Account** with a broker (Zerodha recommended)
